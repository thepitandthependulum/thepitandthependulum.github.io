Symbols

The Statues

The statues in this story represent demons, or sin, pushing the narrator into the pit that he fears.

The Pit

The pit represents Hell, or the place where the condemned fall and reside in agony. The pits were not meant to give the victim peace through death, but rather suffering in life, similarly to hell; "Neither could I forget what I had read of these pits-that the sudden extinction of life formed no part of their most horrible plan"(Poe 172).

General Lasalle

General Lasalle represents a savior- more specifically God, who rescues the sinner (our narrator) from the pits of hell and the demons trying to push him into it.

The Pit and the Pendulum is, at first reading, a plain horror story, but upon deeper reading, it contains several symbols that convey the message of a sinner condemned being rescued by God right before he falls into hell.

Torture

Chinese Water Torture

Victims are restrained on a table, then warm or cold water is dripped slowly on their head. This would slowly drive the victim insane. It was considered "humane" by the Spanish Inquisition.

The Head Crusher

Exactly like it sounds, the Head Crusher literally smashed a victims head. The victim's chin would be placed on a metal bar, and their head under a cap, and the torturer would slowly lower the cap, first crushing a victim's teeth, then the eyes, then eventually the brain.

Strappado

A rope attatched to a pulley would hang a victim by the arms until their shoulders popped out of socket. Jumps and weights were sometimes added for extra pain.

The Rack

Victim's arms and legs would be tied and then pulled forcibly by ropes in opposite directions, this would dislocate and eventually rip off the arms and legs. Seeing a person tortured like this could often make another victim confess.

The Candles

"And then my vision fell on upon the seven tall candles upon the table. At first they wore the aspect of charity and seemed white, slender angels who would save me; but then all at once there came a nausea over my spirit, and I felt every fiber in my frame thrill as if I had touched the wire of a galvanic battery, while the angel forms became meaningless specters, with heads of flame, and I saw that from them there would be no help" (Poe 168).

The Pendulum

"I now observed-with what horror it is needless to say-that its nether extremity was formed of a crescent of glittering steel about a foot in length from horn to horn; the horns upward, and the under edge evidently as keen as that of a razor. Like a razor also, it seemed massy and heavy, tapering from the edge into a solid and broad structure above. It was appended to a weighty rod of brass and the whole hissed as it swung through the air" (Poe 173).

The Statues

"The figures of fiends in aspects of menace with skeleton forms, and other more really fearful images overspread and disfigured the walls" (Poe 172)

"I have observed that, althought the outlines of the figures upon the walls were sufficiently distinct, yet the colors seemed blurred and indefinite. These colors had now assumed, and were momentarily assuming, a starling and most intense brilliancy that gave to the spectral and fiendish portraitures an aspect that might have thrilled even firmer nerves than my own. Demon eyes of a wild and ghastly vivacity glared upon me in a thousand directions where none had been visible before, and gleamed with the lurid luster of a fire that I could not force my imagination to regard as unreal" (Poe 176).
